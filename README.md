# Final Project Rubric

Purpose:

To answer a data driven question by deploying the data science lifecycle. At the end of the process, you will have an appreciation for what it is like to develop your own questions, match these questions with data, test the ability of the data via a model to answer your question, develop a final solution and the articulation of the weaknesses and benefits of the approach. The goal is for you to pull all the skills together that we have worked on all semester, essentially living the life of a practicing data scientist. This is also a great way to start developing a data project portfolio on your repo or website.

Tasks:

Work with your groups to develop and answer a discrete question related to a dataset of your choosing.  Some dataset resources are listed below for potential use. Present a cleanly documented final notebook that walks the reader through your project step by step. This means you need to reference the data science lifecycle and work through each stage deliberately.

Model topics we have covered can be the focus of the final project. Feel free to combine topics, extend them as needed or use more than one to find the best model.

Linear Regression for Prediction
kNN
Clustering – kMeans
Logistic Regression
Decision Trees (Classification or Regression)
Or try Random Forest – lots of Decision Trees
Include the following sections:

Question and background information on the data and why you are asking this question(s). Couple of paragraphs.
Exploratory Data Analysis – Initial summary statistics and graphs with an emphasis on variables you believe to be important for your analysis. Don’t just show a bunch of graphs, you need a story.
Methods – Techniques you are using to address your question and the results of those methods. Training/Tuning phase.
Evaluation of your model – Select appropriate metrics and explain the output as it relates to your question. Testing Phase
Conclusions – What can you say about the results of the methods section as it relates to your question given the limitations to your model.
Team contribution – summary of the various contributions of individual team members – high level it is possible to have people work on the same parts, no problem.
Think about roles (these are just options not required):

Data Engineer – sets up the environment (repo, data pipeline, requirements, codespace, clean and prep data etc.)
Researcher – background and question building
Model builder – Build initial models for testing
“Red Team” – evaluate the model and review the code for quality or performance gaps
Final reviewer – one last run through all the content to ensure quality and clear message.
Technical Notes:

Document what you are doing along the way

Criteria for evaluation:

Oral evaluation will count for 60% of the final grade
Documentation and quality of the materials will be 40%
There will be a collaboration evaluation and if you are viewed as not contributing by your peers you could lose up to two full letter grades.
 

Question Areas for Oral:

Method based – explore the model you selected, how you used it and why you selected the method
